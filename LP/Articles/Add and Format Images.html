Add and change images

 

  1. To add an image to your document, drag the image icon from the Content bar and drop it onto the canvas. This will automatically open up the Image Manager. 

  2. Upload new images by clicking the 'Upload' button, then select an image file and click 'Open'. Alternatively, drag an image file from your desktop and drop it into the editor. Please note that .jpg, .png, .ai, .eps, .tiff, .psd, .pdf, .svg, .bmp are supported.

  3. You should see all uploaded images in the Image Manager under the 'All Images' section. Select the image you would like to add by clicking on its thumbnail. Delete an image from the Image Manager by selecting the thumbnail and clicking the trash icon.

  4. Images will appear in the editor wherever they were dropped. Click and drag the image to alter its location.

  5. To change an image that has already been dropped onto the page, just double-click on the image, and select a new thumbnail.

  6. For more information about managing images, see this tutorial on working with the Image Manager.

 Add image galleries

 

  1. To add an image gallery to your document, drag the image icon from the Content bar and drop it onto the canvas. This will bring up the image manager as seen in the previous section.

  2. Select more than one image and click 'Done'. 

  3. You will now see the 'Slideshow' menu opened in the right pane. Here you can add additional images by clicking the '+'. 

  4. Set the image gallery to fill or fit with the 'Image Size' options in the same 'Slideshow' menu.

  5. Image galleries will appear in the editor wherever they were dropped. Click and drag the gallery to alter its location.

  6. Change the size and shape of the gallery by grabbing a handle in the corner of the gallery and moving it as needed. Alternatively, use the Layout tab of the Context pane to adjust the size manually.

Resize, move, and rotate images

 

  1. Change the size, shape, and rotation of images by grabbing a handle in the corner of the image and moving it as needed. You can also click the text box and use your arrow keys to nudge, or Shift +arrow key to make a leap. Make sure to have the width and height locked you want to maintain the proportions. Alternatively, use the 'Position & Size' options in the Layout tab to re-size manually.

  2. Image proportions are locked by default, but you can link the proportions in 'Position & Size' options.

  3. Revert to an image's original size (of the actual image file) by clicking the 'Original Size' button in the 'Position & Size' options.

  4. To switch out the displayed image, double-click it and select an alternative image from the Image Manager.

Format images

 

  1. Select the image you would like to format.

  2. Change the style, color, or width of the image's border in the Image tab.
  3. To add a drop shadow, toggle the 'Shadow' option to 'On,' and adjust the angle, distance, blur, and opacity of your shadow.
  4. To change the opacity of the image, drag the 'Opacity' slider. When you make a box with text less opaque, your text will fade too.

Modify images (saturation, contrast, brightness)

 

  1. Edit the color balance of an image by clicking the 'Image Effects' button in the Image tab.

  2. Edit saturation, contrast, and brightness with the sliders in the 'Image Effects' panel. Then click 'OK' to apply the changes.

  3. To revert to a previous version of the image, click the undo button in the upper right corner. You can also use Ctrl+Z or Cmd+Z.

Editing the mask of images (cropping and resizing)

 

  1. Editing an image's mask is similar to cropping an image. To edit the mask, right-click on the image and select 'Edit Crop or  Mask' from the Menu bar or from the Image tab.

  2. To change the mask's dimensions, click and drag the black corners. Anything that is faded will not show in the document.

  3. To change the crop points, drag the white squares at the edges of the picture to crop the white frame to its desired size.

  4. Press Enter to save changes or press Escape to cancel the changes. Note: All changes will be lost if you press Escape.
