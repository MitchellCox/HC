Share a document with collaborators via a link or an emailed invitation

  1. To invite collaborators to edit, view, or comment on a document, click the  blue hamburger button on the top right of the page. Next, click 'Share'.

  2. Send an invitation to an individual user, a group on your team, or your entire team by typing the email address or name of the individual or group you want to invite.



  3. If you want your recipients to be able to pass the document to others, click "Access Link," and copy the provided URL. Make sure to check the box next to multi-use link so the link doesn't expire.

  4. Before clicking "Create Access Link", specify the access level you'd like your collaborator(s) to have. The options include 'can view', 'can comment', 'can edit', and 'can edit and share'.

  5. Click  'Generate Link.'

Comment on page elements

  1. Comment on specific elements within a document by right-clicking on the element (e.g. shape, text box, text selection, image, gallery, or video), then select 'Add Comment'. You can also select an object, then add a comment from the 'Insert' menu in the Menu bar.
  2. Enter your comment into the field that appears.
  3. Reply to a comment by selecting the quotation marks on the element selected on the page. Type your reply and press Enter.  
  4. Edit comments by selecting them. 
  5. Delete a comment thread by selecting it, then clicking 'Resolve'.
  6. Click on a comment to see its associated object or text.

Mention team members in comments

If you are on a Team, you can notify a teammate of the comment by typing @ + any of the letters in the user's name, and selecting the user's email address from the prompt.

Chat with collaborators

  1. The chat area is on the lower-left side of the editor. This is where you can communicate in real time with collaborators on the document.
  2. You will see a colored icon next to Chat, which indicates your presence. If any collaborators are logged in, you will see additional icons.
  3. Click 'Chat' to enter chat mode. 
  4. Enter your message and press Enter to send.  
  5. You can hide the chat box by clicking the minimize icon on the upper-right side of the box.
  6. When a collaborator sends you a message, the chat box will open automatically.

Document presence

  1. When multiple collaborators are present in a document, document presence will show you where they are currently working.
  2. When they select an element, you'll see a border around that element. The color of the border corresponds to the color of the collaborator's icon in the chat area.
