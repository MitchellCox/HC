Swim lanes divide your flowcharts into meaningful sections. Among other things, they can be used to sort responsibilities by person or to separate a process into phases. When you place an object in a swim lane, it stays there, even if you move the lane.

Adding and Customizing Swim Lanes
  1. Open the Shape Library
  2. In the left column under "Standard" make sure that “Flowchart Shapes” has been selected, and press “Save.”
    1. In the left hand toolbar, find the “Containers” library and select the horizontal or vertical swim lane. You can see shape labels by hovering your mouse over a shape.
    2. Select the shape, drag and drop it onto your canvas.
    3. You can change a label by double clicking on the header.
    4. Click and drag the borders to resize.
Add/Delete, Re-Orient or Move Lanes

When you click on the border of your swim lane, a panel should appear to the side of your canvas.

  1. Add or remove lanes by changing the number of lanes listed in the panel.
  2. You can change lane orientation by selecting from the drop-down lists in the panel. When you re-orient a lane, all objects in the lane will re-orient as well.
  3. To move a lane, right click on the border or header of the lane. Select “Move Lane Up” or down or right or left depending on your desired direction.
Resizing Swim Lanes
  1. Click on the border of a lane and drag your mouse to resize the lane.
Styling to Swim Lanes
Swim lanes can be styled using the top toolbar and the graphic panel of the right toolbar.
  1. Edit text font, size and color
  2. Change line color, thickness and pattern
  3. Change fill color and texture
  4. Add a shadow
