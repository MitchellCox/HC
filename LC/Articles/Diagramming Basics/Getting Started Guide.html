This tutorial explains the basic components of what you see when you open a document — the Canvas, Menu bar, Context pane, Content bar, Page thumbnails, and the blue buttons above the Menu Bar. For practice, try our Interactive Tutorial.

Canvas

The canvas is the heart of the editor. This is where you drag objects to create beautiful print and digital documents. In the Editor, you can:

  • Change the title of your document
  • Customize background styling
  • Right-click to add vertical and horizontal alignment guides
  • Edit and resize elements

In your Preferences under the Lucid icon on the Menu bar, you can:

  • Set the default Zoom to Normal (125%), Page Width, or a Full Page
  • Turn hints on/off
  • Show or hide the Guides
  • Snap to Guides, Grid, or both
  • Show or hide the Grid
  • Set default Ruler units (Inches, Centimeters, Pixels, or Points)
  • Set Default page units (Inches, Centimeters, Pixels, or Points)
  • Enable Iconfinder or Google Search

 

Menu Bar
Located at the top of the editor, the Menu bar provides access to most common actions and objects. You can find most options in the Editor duplicated here with keyboard shortcuts, known as hotkeys, within each drop-down menu.
 
Context Pane

The Context pane to the right contains all of the settings you will need to edit your documents. The Context pane consists of two main tabs (Page and Layout) with additional tabs (Text, Style, Scroll Area, Gallery, Image, Video, Shape) opening upon selection of relevant content. At the bottom of the Context pane is the Layers pane, which will show you an ordered breakdown of each object on the page.

  • Page - The Page tab allows you to change size & orientation, header & footer, margins, and background.
  • Layout - The Layout tab allows you to change the position, size, and rotation of objects as well as their arrangement in the document. You can now use the align, match size, and distribution options to make objects the same size, align them to a certain section of the document, or distribute along a horizontal or vertical line.
  • Text - The Text tab allows you to change paragraph styles, font, color, formatting, size, alignment, lists, spacing and padding, columns, tabs & indentations, horizontal rules, warp, and hyphenation. To view all options, just click the down arrow.
  • Style - The Style tab opens in conjunction with the Text tab and only works on text boxes. The Style tab allows you to change the fill of a text box to a color, gradient, or image, and change the border color. You can also change the shadow and opacity of the text box.
  • Scroll Area - The Scroll Area tab allows you to change the background color, margins, and bottom padding of a scrolling area. You can drag content onto the scrollable area just as you would on the rest of the page. This feature is for digital documents only.
  • Image - The Image tab allows you to scale, stretch, tile, or crop the image. It also gives you options to change the saturation, contrast, brightness, the border color, the rotation or shadow of the image, and the opacity of the image.
  • Video - At present, the Video tab only shows a place to insert the URL of your video, but we will include more options here down the road. To use your own video content, you must first upload your video to YouTube or another storage site, such as Dropbox, with privacy set as either public or unlisted.
  • Shapes - The Shapes tab allows you to change the fill, border color, shadow, and opacity of the object. Choose from a variety of shapes to add to the canvas.
  • Layers pane - The Layers pane will show you a breakdown of the objects on each page in order. You can hide or show various shapes by clicking the 'eye' icon to the left of the layer's name.
Content Bar
To the far left of the Editor, you will see what we call the Content bar. It contains the icons for the various objects you can add to your document. Just click and drag an icon to bring an object to the Canvas.
 
Page Thumbnail

To the left of the Editor, just before the Content bar, you'll see page thumbnails where you can easily navigate and change the order of pages in your document.

  • Right-click to delete or duplicate pages.

  • Click on a page thumbnail to navigate to that page.

  • Drag and drop pages to change the order in which they appear.

Blue Button at the Top Right

At the top right of the Editor, click on the blue hamburger button. you will find three mode buttons. You can switch between modes to access the following actions:

  • Preview - Click to check out how your document will look if you publish it now.

  • Share - Click this button to share your document with others inside Lucidpress. You can also publish your document to the web, or distribute your document on other sites.

  • Download - Click this button to download a jpg, png, or pdf of your document.
