Learn how to create an eye-catching presentation and maintain the attention of your audience as you present your flowchart one piece at a time.

To Start
  1. Open a document and create a flowchart.
  2. Click on the slide presenter icon on the right-side toolbar, and you'll see a panel that looks like this:

Here is a quick breakdown:

  • The “eye” icon at the top lets you hide any slides you create.
  • The # shows you the order the slides will follow in your presentation.
  • “Page” shows the name of the page each slide is on.
  • “Slide Name” displays the unique name you give the slide.
  • Click the “Play” button to start a presentation from a selected slide.
  • Multi-select a number of objects, click (+ Slide), and we’ll create a new slide around those objects.
  • While you have a slide selected, clicking (+ Slide) will duplicate that slide.
  • Click (+ Highlight) to make the object surrounding your selection fade during a presentation.
  • Click the gear icon to set default Highlight transition times and an Aspect ratio.

Note: This timer does not allow you to autoplay a presentation. At present, all presentations require a click or arrow key to move back and forth along the created path.

To Add a Presentation
  1. Select one or more objects and click (+ Slide) to add a slide at the default aspect ratio.

  2. Resize a slide by dragging at the corners.

    Note: For the best viewing experience, try to shape your custom slides in the same general shape as your screen. We help you by fitting slides to your personal screen, so you might see a little more content than you selected if your slides are square.

  3. To emphasize elements of a slide, click on a slide then hold down “Shift” to select the shapes on the slide that you want to accent. Click the highlight button. You’ll see the selected shapes glow with the same orange as the presentation slide.

  4. If you want to add another shape to the highlight, just click your Slide again, Shift-click the additional object, and press the “Update Highlight” button.

    Note: These images are from a different document.

  5. If you want to remove all highlights, just click the slide and choose “- Highlight”.

  6. If you want to remove the highlight from just a few shapes in a slide, just Shift-click the desired shapes ONCE and press the “Update Highlight” button again. You won’t see an immediate change (which could be confusing), but clicking once and updating will do the trick.

  7. We add a “flash” transition to highlights, but at least in our office it seems to be a “love it or hate it” thing. To turn it off, click the gear icon and set the default transition time to 0.

  8. Click “Present” and you’ll see your presentation on the big screen!
    Note: For optimal viewing, make sure the zoom settings in your browser are set to default.

  9. Click the arrow keys at the bottom of the screen to move back & forth, zoom, go fullscreen, and turn on or off hotspots, page tabs, and notes.

        

Now you are ready to astound your colleagues with your impressive visual presentations!
