Presentation mode is a helpful tool that allows you to display your charts in smaller, piece-by-piece slides that flow throughout your diagram. This helpful tool can break-down your diagrams for your viewers. See it in action:

Create Slides from your Document
  1. Open a document that you wish to display in presentation mode.
  2. Click the "Present" Tab on the right toolbar to open the presentation mode settings.
  3. Click the gear icon to set the aspect ratio and the duration you would like your slides to show for.
  4. Select one or more objects in your document and click +Slide to create your first slide.
  5. Resize the slide by dragging the corners. Move your slide by dragging the top-left corner of the box.
  6. Repeat this process for each slide you want to create.

Present and Export your Slides to Google Slides

Click the "Present" button on the bottom-right side of your screen to present the slides you made. You can toggle between slides using the arrow keys on your keyboard. Otherwise, the slides will change at the intervals you set earlier.

You can export your slides to Google Slides by clicking "Send to Google Slides". A dialogue box will pop-up and, if you allow access, your slides will be available in your Google Drive Folder.

The first slide of your Google Presentation is an editable page displaying the original name of your Lucidchart document. Each additional slide will inherit the slide's title from Lucidchart (you can edit these in the Google Slide Presentation). 

Troubleshooting the Google Slides Integration

If you experience issues exporting your Lucidchart diagram to Google Slides, try the following steps:

  1. Make sure you're signed into your Google Account when prompted. Assure you're checking the correct Google Drive Account for your slides.
  2. Assure you've clicked "Allow" when prompted to give Lucidchart access to your Google Slides documents.
  3. You have a strong and reliable internet connection.

If you continue to experience issues, try unlinking your account from Google Drive then relinking it. You can do this here if you're already signed into your Lucidchart account.
